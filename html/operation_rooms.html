Operation Rooms

In the operating room The operating room provides a sterile environment in which the surgical team can perform the surgery. The surgical team consists of the following: Chief Surgeon: He directs surgery One or more assistant surgeons: assist the principal surgeon Anesthesiologist: controls the anesthesia process and closely monitors the patient Surgeon's assistant nurse: Passes tools to surgeons Ambulatory nurse: Provides additional equipment to the surgical teem. The operating room usually includes a monitor that displays vital signs, an instrument table, and a lamp that illuminates the area of ​​the operation. Anesthetic gases are pumped into tubes into the anesthesia machine, and a catheter attached to a suction machine removes excess blood and other fluids that can prevent surgeons from clearly seeing the tissue. For intravenous fluids that are started before the patient enters the operating room, doctors continue to administer them
